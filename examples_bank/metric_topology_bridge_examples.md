# Metric Topology Bridge Examples - v1.0.298

This examples-bank surface records small, original, testable contracts connecting metric data with topological conclusions.

## Contract MTB-01: equivalent finite metrics

If two explicit finite metrics are supplied together with a witness that their induced open-set structures coincide, the package may mark the equivalence as exact.  Without such a witness or theorem assumption, the correct status is `unknown`.

## Contract MTB-02: bounded metric transforms

For an explicit finite metric `d`, the transforms `d/(1+d)` and `min(1,d)` preserve metric validity.  The example surface treats them as metric contracts, not as copied textbook exercises.

## Contract MTB-03: product metrics

Finite product metric checks must record the factor count, product carrier size, and chosen metric mode (`max`, `sum`, or `euclidean`).

## Contract MTB-04: topology versus metric data

A topological conclusion that needs metric data must not be fabricated when only a symbolic metric label is present.


## Contract MTB-05: metric split-axis discipline

Chapter 15 examples must label whether they serve the early metric-intuition lane or the advanced metric-structure lane.  A finite open-ball example belongs to the metric-intuition lane; Cauchy/completeness warnings, equivalent-metric comparisons, and product/function metric preparation belong to the advanced lane unless the task explicitly only asks for basic ball reading.
