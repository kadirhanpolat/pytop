Operads

Operads: symmetric/non-symmetric operads, Koszul duality, bar-cobar constructions, A-infinity/L-infinity/E-infinity algebras, little disks operads, tree compositions.

Key theorems and constructions implemented

  • Operad (symmetric): a symmetric operad P is a collection {P(n)}_{n>=0} of S_n-modules (right modules over the symmetric group S_n) together with composition maps: gamma: P(k) tensor P(n_1) tensor … tensor P(n_k) -> P(n_1 + … + n_k), a unit 1 in P(1), subject to associativity, unity, and equivariance axioms. Equivalently, P is a monoid in the category (Seq, tensor_circ, I) of symmetric sequences with respect to the composition product: (P circ Q)(n) = direct_sum_{k>=0} P(k) tensor_{S_k} Q^{tensor k}(n). An algebra over P is a vector space (or object) A together with maps P(n) tensor_{S_n} A^{tensor n} -> A satisfying unit and associativity with respect to the operad structure. Key examples: - Ass: the associative operad with Ass(n) = k[S_n] (free S_n-module of rank 1);

    Ass-algebras are exactly associative algebras.

    • Com: the commutative operad with Com(n) = k (trivial S_n-module of rank 1); Com-algebras are exactly commutative associative algebras.

    • Lie: the Lie operad encoding the Lie bracket; Lie-algebras are Lie algebras.

    Non-symmetric (planar) operad: same as above but without the S_n-action; algebras are algebras with ordered inputs. Non-symmetric Ass encodes associative algebras; non-symmetric Com does not make sense (commutativity requires the transposition).

  • Koszul duality for operads (Ginzburg-Kapranov, 1994): a binary quadratic operad P (generated by binary operations with quadratic relations) has a Koszul dual operad P^!. A quadratic operad P = (E, R) is presented by generators E in arity 2 and quadratic relations R in E circ E. The Koszul dual: P^! = (E^*, R^perp) where E^* = Hom(E, k) with twisted S_2-action, and R^perp is the orthogonal complement of R under the natural pairing. Fundamental examples: - Com^! = Lie, Lie^! = Com (Com and Lie are Koszul dual to each other). - Ass^! = Ass (associative operad is self-dual up to twist). Koszul operads: P is Koszul if the augmentation epsilon: B(P^!) -> k is a quasi-isomorphism, where B denotes the bar construction. Equivalently, the Koszul complex P^! circ_kappa P is acyclic (quasi-isomorphic to the unit operad I). Consequence: if P is Koszul, then P^{infty} = Omega(P^!) (bar-cobar construction) is the minimal resolution of P, whose algebras are P-algebras up to homotopy (P_infty-algebras). The main theorem: Ass, Com, and Lie are all Koszul operads.

  • Bar-cobar constructions: for an augmented operad P (with augmentation P -> I), the bar construction B(P) is a cooperad (graded, with a differential making it a dg-cooperad) with B(P) = T^c(s P_+) (cofree cooperad on the suspension of the augmentation ideal). The cobar construction Omega(C) of an augmented cooperad C is the operad Omega(C) = T(s^{-1} C_+) with differential induced by the coproduct of C. Adjunction: (Omega, B) form an adjoint pair between dg-operads and dg-cooperads. The counit Omega(B(P)) -> P is a quasi-isomorphism for augmented operads (always), and the unit P^! -> B(P)/relations is a quasi-isomorphism iff P is Koszul. Applications: the bar construction B(P) is used to define P-infinity algebras (via their Maurer-Cartan elements), and the Koszul complex is the twisted composite.

  • A-infinity algebras (Stasheff, 1963): an A-infinity algebra (A_infty-algebra) is a graded vector space A with a collection of multilinear maps {m_n: A^{tensor n} -> A}_{n>=1} of degree 2-n satisfying the A_infty-relations: sum_{n=r+s+t} (-1)^{rs+t} m_{r+1+t}(id^r tensor m_s tensor id^t) = 0 for all n>=1. For n=1: m_1 circ m_1 = 0 (m_1 is a differential); for n=2: m_1 m_2 = m_2(m_1 tensor 1 + 1 tensor m_1) (derivation condition); for n=3: the failure of m_2 to be associative is controlled by m_3 (homotopy). If m_n = 0 for n>=3: A_infty reduces to a dg-algebra (differential graded associative). If additionally m_1 = 0: A_infty reduces to a graded associative algebra. A_infty-algebras are the algebras over the A_infty operad = Omega(Ass^!). Homotopy transfer theorem: any A_infty algebra structure on a complex (A, d) can be transferred to any homotopy retract of A, producing an A_infty structure on the cohomology H^*(A). This is fundamental in homological perturbation theory.

  • L-infinity algebras: an L_infty-algebra (L_infty = strong homotopy Lie algebra) is a graded vector space L with maps {l_n: Lambda^n L -> L}_{n>=1} (alternating, degree 2-n) satisfying generalized Jacobi identities: sum_{i+j=n+1} sum_{sigma} (-1)^sigma (-1)^{i(j-1)} l_j(l_i(x_{sigma(1)},…), …) = 0. For n=1: l_1^2 = 0 (differential); for n=2: Jacobi up to homotopy controlled by l_3. L_infty-algebras are the algebras over L_infty = Omega(Lie^!) = Omega(Com). Applications: formal deformation theory (Maurer-Cartan elements of a dgla control deformations), derived geometry (L_infty-algebras arise as tangent complexes of derived stacks), and string field theory (L_infty-structure on closed string field theory). Homotopy transfer: L_infty structures transfer along homotopy retracts; every dg-Lie algebra is L_infty-quasi-isomorphic to its cohomology (with transferred structure).

  • Little disks operads E_n (Boardman-Vogt, May): the little n-disks operad D_n has D_n(k) = space of k disjoint little n-dimensional disks embedded in the unit disk D^n (rectilinearly, i.e., affine embeddings). Composition: substitute little disks into disks. E_n = D_n as an operad in topological spaces (or its homology H_*(D_n)). Key properties: - E_1 ≃ Ass (over k, H_*(E_1) ≅ Ass): E_1-algebras ≅ A_infty-algebras. - E_infty = colim_n E_n ≃ Com (over k): E_infty-algebras ≅ strictly commutative algebras up to homotopy. - E_n-algebras interpolate between A_infty (n=1) and E_infty (n=infty). Cohomology of configuration spaces: H_*(D_n(k)) = Poisson_n(k) operad (for n>=2). Recognition principle (May): a connected (n-1)-fold loop space Omega^{n-1}X is group-complete E_n-algebra. The delooping machine: E_n-algebras are equivalent (up to group completion) to n-fold loop spaces. Factorization homology (Ayala-Francis): for M an n-manifold, the factorization homology int_M A of an E_n-algebra A is a topological invariant, generalizing Hochschild homology.

pytop.operads.is_koszul_operad(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the operad is Koszul.

An augmented quadratic operad P is Koszul if the Koszul complex P^! circ_kappa P is acyclic (quasi-isomorphic to the unit operad I). Equivalently, the bar construction B(P) is quasi-isomorphic to the Koszul dual cooperad P^!. Key Koszul operads: Ass, Com, Lie, E_n.

Decision layers

  1. Explicit Koszul tags -> true.

  2. Known Koszul operads (Ass, Com, Lie, E_n by name/tag) -> true.

  3. Non-quadratic or known non-Koszul operads -> false.

  4. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.operads.has_infinity_algebra_structure(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the space admits a homotopy algebra structure.

A P_infty-algebra (A_infty, L_infty, C_infty, E_infty, …) is an algebra over the minimal resolution P_infty = Omega(P^!) of a Koszul operad P. Key cases: A_infty (homotopy associative), L_infty (homotopy Lie), E_infty (commutative up to all higher homotopy coherence).

Decision layers

  1. Explicit infinity-algebra tags (a_infinity, l_infinity, …) -> true.

  2. Bar-cobar construction tags (which produce minimal resolutions) -> true.

  3. Strictly algebras over Koszul operads (which have infinity versions) -> true.

  4. Strict (non-homotopy) only tags -> false.

  5. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.operads.admits_koszul_dual(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the operad has a well-defined Koszul dual.

The Koszul dual P^! of a binary quadratic operad P = F(E)/(R) is defined as P^! = F(E^*[1])/(R^perp). The Koszul dual is well-defined for binary quadratic operads. Examples: Com^! = Lie, Lie^! = Com, Ass^! = Ass (up to twist).

Decision layers

  1. Explicit Koszul duality tags -> true.

  2. Known binary quadratic operads (Ass, Com, Lie) -> true.

  3. Non-quadratic / colored / modular operads (no standard Koszul dual) -> false.

  4. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.operads.is_binary_quadratic_operad(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the operad is binary quadratic.

A binary quadratic operad is generated by binary operations (arity-2 generators E) with quadratic relations (R subset E circ E, involving two binary operations composed in arity 3). This class includes Ass, Com, Lie, Poisson, pre-Lie, and many others. Binary quadratic operads are the natural input for Koszul duality.

Decision layers

  1. Explicit binary quadratic or tree composition tags -> true.

  2. Known binary quadratic operads (Ass, Com, Lie) -> true.

  3. Higher arity, colored, or modular operads -> false.

  4. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.operads.associahedron_complex(n: int) → Any

Stasheff associahedron K_n as a simplicial complex.

Vertices = full binary trees with n leaves (Catalan number C_{n-1}). 1-simplices = pairs connected by one tree rotation. K₃ = interval (2 vertices, 1 edge). K₄ = pentagon (5 vertices, 5 edges, homeomorphic to S¹).

Parameters

n: number of leaves (n ≥ 2)

Returns

SimplicialComplex whose 1-skeleton is the rotation graph of K_n.

Examples

>>> K4 = associahedron_complex(4)
>>> sum(1 for s in K4.simplexes if len(s.vertices) == 1)  # 5 vertices
5
pytop.operads.operad_composition_check(mu: list[list[int]]) → dict[str, Any]

Verify the operad associativity axiom for a binary composition map.

The composition µ: A ⊗ A → A (given as a matrix µ[i][j] = index of µ(eᵢ, eⱼ)) must satisfy the associativity pentagon: µ(µ(a,b), c) = µ(a, µ(b,c)).

Parameters

mu: square n×n matrix of non-negative integers (< n); mu[i][j] is the

index of µ(eᵢ, eⱼ) in the basis

Returns

dict with associative, n_violations, is_operad_composition.

Examples

Constant composition µ(i,j) = 0 is trivially associative:

>>> operad_composition_check([[0, 0], [0, 0]])["associative"]
True
pytop.operads.bar_construction_sc(n_generators: int, n_relations: int = 0) → Any

Bar construction B(A) of an algebra with given generators and relations.

The bar complex B(A) has B(A)_k = A^{⊗k+2}. Its 1-skeleton over the generators produces a complete graph; relations introduce 2-simplices.

Parameters

n_generators: number of algebra generators (= dimension of A) n_relations: number of relations (introduces 2-simplices for the first

n_relations consecutive generator triples)

Returns

SimplicialComplex with n_generators vertices, edges for each pair, and 2-simplices for compatible relation triples.

Examples

Free algebra on 2 generators (no relations):

>>> sc = bar_construction_sc(2, 0)
>>> sum(1 for s in sc.simplexes if len(s.vertices) == 1)
2
