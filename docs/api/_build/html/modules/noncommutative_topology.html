Noncommutative Topology

Noncommutative topology: C*-algebras, Gelfand duality, K-theory, spectral triples, and the noncommutative geometry programme of Connes.

Key theorems implemented

  • Gelfand duality (1943): there is a contravariant equivalence of categories between compact Hausdorff spaces and commutative unital C*-algebras, given by X |-> C(X) (continuous complex-valued functions) and A |-> Spec(A) (maximal ideal space / character space). Under this duality: points of X <-> characters chi: A -> C; open sets of X <-> closed ideals of C(X); continuous maps f: X -> Y <-> -homomorphisms f: C(Y) -> C(X). A noncommutative C*-algebra is thus a ‘noncommutative topological space’. Key examples: C(S^1) = commutative (S^1 is classical); M_n(C) = noncommutative (n x n matrices, no classical Gelfand dual).

  • Gelfand-Naimark theorem (1943): every C*-algebra A is isometrically -isomorphic to a closed *-subalgebra of B(H), the bounded operators on some Hilbert space H. Commutative case: A ≅ C_0(X) for locally compact Hausdorff X = Spec(A). This justifies calling noncommutative C-algebras ‘noncommutative spaces’.

  • K-theory for C*-algebras (Atiyah 1964 / Hirzebruch): for a C*-algebra A, K_0(A) is the Grothendieck group of Murray-von Neumann equivalence classes of projections in M_infty(A). K_1(A) = K_0(SA) where SA = C_0((0,1)) tensor A is the suspension. The six-term exact sequence and Bott periodicity K_{n+2}(A) ≅ K_n(A) are the main computational tools. For commutative A = C(X): K_0(C(X)) = K^0(X) (topological K-theory), K_1(C(X)) = K^1(X) (Atiyah-Hirzebruch). Key example: K_0(M_n(C)) = Z, K_1(M_n(C)) = 0 (matrix algebras).

  • Connes’ spectral triple (A, H, D) (1994): a spectral triple (also called an unbounded Fredholm module) consists of a *-algebra A represented on a Hilbert space H, and a self-adjoint operator D (‘Dirac operator’) with compact resolvent, such that [D, a] is bounded for all a in A. The spectral triple encodes a noncommutative Riemannian geometry: the metric on the state space S(A) is d(phi, psi) = sup{|phi(a) - psi(a)| : ||[D,a]|| <= 1}. For commutative A = C^infty(M): take H = L^2(M, S) (spinor bundle), D = Dirac operator — recovers classical Riemannian geometry. The spectral triple is even (graded) if H has a Z/2Z grading chi with chi·D = -D·chi, modelling the chirality of the Dirac operator.

  • Morita equivalence: two C*-algebras A and B are Morita equivalent if there is an A-B imprimitivity bimodule — a full Hilbert bimodule. Morita equivalent algebras have the same K-theory and representation theory. Key fact: M_n(A) is Morita equivalent to A for all n >= 1 (the Morita invariance of K-theory). The noncommutative torus A_theta is Morita equivalent to A_{theta’} when theta and theta’ are related by a modular transformation in PSL(2,Z).

  • Noncommutative torus A_theta (Rieffel 1981): for theta in [0,1], the noncommutative torus A_theta is the universal C*-algebra generated by unitaries U, V with VU = e^{2 pi i theta} UV. For theta = 0: A_0 = C(T^2) (commutative). For theta irrational: A_theta is a simple C*-algebra. K_0(A_theta) = Z^2, K_1(A_theta) = Z^2 (same as C(T^2) by Pimsner-Voiculescu). The noncommutative torus is the paradigm example of a noncommutative manifold and models the quantum Hall effect.

  • Nuclear C*-algebras: A is nuclear if there is a unique C*-cross-norm on A tensor B for every C*-algebra B (equivalently, the minimal and maximal tensor products agree). Commutative C*-algebras, AF-algebras, and the CAR algebra are nuclear. The Connes embedding problem (resolved negatively, Ji-Natarajan-Vidick-Wright-Yuen 2020): not every II_1 factor embeds in the ultrapower of the hyperfinite II_1 factor R.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.is_commutative_cstar(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the C*-algebra is commutative (has a classical Gelfand dual).

By Gelfand’s theorem (1943), a commutative C*-algebra A is isomorphic to C_0(X) where X = Spec(A) is the locally compact Hausdorff space of characters. A is unital iff X is compact. Key facts: - C(X) for compact Hausdorff X is the archetypal commutative C*-algebra. - Group C*-algebra C*(G) is commutative iff G is abelian. - All commutative C*-algebras are nuclear. - Matrix algebras M_n(C) (n >= 2) and the noncommutative torus are NOT commutative.

Decision layers

  1. Explicit ‘commutative_cstar’ or ‘abelian_cstar’ or ‘c_of_x’ tag -> true.

  2. Gelfand dual / character space tag -> true.

  3. Noncommutative tags (matrix, noncommutative_torus, cuntz, compact_operators) -> false.

  4. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.is_nuclear_cstar(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the C*-algebra is nuclear.

A C*-algebra A is nuclear if the minimal (spatial) tensor product A tensor_min B equals the maximal tensor product A tensor_max B for every C*-algebra B. Key facts: - All commutative C*-algebras are nuclear. - All finite-dimensional C*-algebras (matrix algebras, direct sums) are nuclear. - AF-algebras, Cuntz algebras, and the noncommutative torus are nuclear. - B(H) (all bounded operators) is NOT nuclear for dim(H) = infty. - C*(G) is nuclear iff G is amenable (Day’s theorem). - Nuclear = amenable in the C*-algebra sense (Connes, Haagerup).

Decision layers

  1. Explicit ‘nuclear_cstar’ or nuclear-inducing tags -> true.

  2. Non-nuclear tags (full group algebra of non-amenable group, B(H)) -> false.

  3. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.is_simple_cstar(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the C*-algebra is simple (no proper closed two-sided ideals).

A C*-algebra A is simple if its only closed two-sided ideals are {0} and A itself. Key facts: - M_n(C) is simple for all n >= 1. - A_theta (irrational rotation algebra) is simple for theta irrational. - Cuntz algebras O_n are purely infinite simple. - K(H) is simple (as a non-unital C*-algebra). - C(X) is simple iff X is a single point. - AF-algebras are generically NOT simple (unless they are the CAR algebra or similar).

Decision layers

  1. Explicit ‘simple_cstar’ or known simple classes -> true.

  2. Commutative algebra over non-trivial space, or AF-algebra -> false.

  3. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.has_gelfand_dual(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the C*-algebra has a classical Gelfand dual (is commutative).

By Gelfand duality, a C*-algebra A has a classical Hausdorff space as its ‘noncommutative space’ iff A is commutative. The Gelfand dual is Spec(A) = the space of characters (unital *-homomorphisms A -> C). For noncommutative A, Spec(A) is empty or trivial, and the ‘space’ is purely virtual (noncommutative geometry).

Decision layers

  1. Explicit ‘gelfand_dual’ or commutative tags -> true.

  2. Noncommutative or no-Gelfand tags -> false.

  3. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.has_spectral_triple(space: Any) → Result

Check whether the algebra carries a spectral triple (Connes’ noncommutative geometry).

A spectral triple (A, H, D) encodes a noncommutative Riemannian geometry: - A: a -algebra (or C-algebra) acting on a Hilbert space H - D: an unbounded self-adjoint operator on H (the ‘Dirac operator’) with compact

resolvent (1+D^2)^{-1} in K(H), and [D, a] bounded for all a in A.

The spectral triple is even if there is a grading operator chi with chi^2 = 1, chi D = -D chi, chi a = a chi. Key examples: - (C^infty(M), L^2(M, S), D_M): the classical Dirac operator on a spin^c manifold M. - (A_theta, L^2(A_theta)^2, D): the noncommutative torus spectral triple. - (M_n(C), C^n, D): finite spectral triples encoding finite metric spaces.

Decision layers

  1. Explicit ‘spectral_triple’ or Dirac operator tag -> true.

  2. Known classes (spin^c manifold, noncommutative torus, matrix algebra) -> true.

  3. No-spectral-triple or purely-infinite algebras -> false.

  4. Unknown otherwise.

pytop.noncommutative_topology.k0_group_matrix_algebra(n: int) → dict[str, Any]

K₀ group of the matrix algebra M_n(ℚ).

By Morita invariance of K-theory: K₀(M_n(ℚ)) ≅ K₀(ℚ) ≅ ℤ. The generator is [P₁], the class of a rank-1 projection in M_n(ℚ). The identity [I_n] = n · [P₁] in K₀(M_n(ℚ)).

Parameters

n: matrix size (n ≥ 1)

Returns

dict with K0_rank, generator_class, identity_class, morita_equivalent_to_Q, description.

Examples

>>> k0_group_matrix_algebra(3)["identity_class"]
3
>>> k0_group_matrix_algebra(1)["K0_rank"]
1
pytop.noncommutative_topology.spectral_dimension_finite(eigenvalues: list[float], threshold: float = 1e-10) → dict[str, Any]

Estimate spectral dimension d_s from eigenvalue growth of a Dirac operator.

The spectral dimension satisfies Tr(e^{-tD²}) ~ t^{-d_s/2} as t → 0⁺. By Karamata’s theorem this is equivalent to the eigenvalue counting function N(λ) ~ C · λ^{d_s/2}, so we fit d_s via log-log linear regression on {(log λ_k, log k)} pairs (positive eigenvalues only).

Parameters

eigenvalues: non-negative eigenvalues of |D| or D² (need not be sorted) threshold: eigenvalues below this are considered zero and excluded

Returns

dict with spectral_dimension, n_positive_eigenvalues, slope.

Examples

Flat torus (2D): Weyl law gives N(λ) ~ λ, so d_s ≈ 2:

>>> evs = list(range(1, 50))
>>> abs(spectral_dimension_finite(evs)["spectral_dimension"] - 2.0) < 0.5
True
pytop.noncommutative_topology.k1_group_matrix_algebra(n: int) → dict[str, Any]

K₁ group of the matrix algebra M_n(ℚ).

By Morita invariance: K₁(M_n(ℚ)) ≅ K₁(ℚ) ≅ GL₁(ℚ)^{ab} = ℚ*. The abelian group ℚ* ≅ ℤ/2 × ⊕_p ℤ (sign × p-adic valuations). The determinant det: GL_n(ℚ)^{ab} → ℚ* is an isomorphism (Whitehead lemma).

Parameters

n: matrix size (n ≥ 1)

Returns

dict with K1_description, torsion_part, free_part_description, determinant_isomorphism, morita_invariant, description.

Examples

>>> k1_group_matrix_algebra(2)["torsion_part"]
'ℤ/2'
>>> k1_group_matrix_algebra(5)["morita_invariant"]
True
